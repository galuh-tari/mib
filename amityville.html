AMITYVILLE

A Healthcare Data Analyst accesses the system to monitor and evaluate hospital performance across the state using key performance indicators (KPIs), enabling data-driven decision making and quality improvement.

    • SMD

    Screening for metabolic disorders measures the percentage of patients discharged from an inpatient psychiatric facility with a prescription for one or more routinely scheduled antipsychotic medications for whom a structured screening for metabolic indicators was completed during their stay or in the 12 months prior to discharge.

    FAPH

    FAPH-30: Follow-up after psychiatric hospitalization (30 days) measures the percentage of inpatient psychiatric facility hospitalizations for treatment of select mental health or substance use disorders that were followed by an outpatient mental health care or substance use disorder encounter within 30 days of discharge. FAPH-7: Follow-up after psychiatric hospitalization (7 days) measures the same population as FAPH-30 but tracks whether an outpatient mental health or substance use encounter occurred within the first 7 days after discharge, reflecting a stricter and more clinically critical window. An outpatient visit with a mental health practitioner after discharge is recommended to ensure that the patient's transition to the home or work environment is supported and that gains made during hospitalization are not lost, and it also helps providers detect early post-hospitalization reactions or medication problems.

    MedCont

    Medication continuation measures the percentage of psychiatric patients admitted to an inpatient psychiatric facility for major depressive disorder, schizophrenia, or bipolar disorder who filled a prescription for an evidence-based medication within 2 days prior to discharge through 30 days post-discharge. PubMed Central staying on medication after leaving the hospital is essential because guidelines from the American Psychiatric Association emphasize the importance of treatment adherence and uninterrupted medication regimens to prevent symptoms and relapse.

    READM

    30-Day Readmission Rate Measures the percentage of patients who were readmitted to any acute care hospital within 30 days of discharge from a psychiatric facility. The measure is risk-standardized, meaning each facility's rate accounts for differences in patient population, and each facility's result is compared against the national rate and evaluated as better than, no different than, or worse than the national rate.
